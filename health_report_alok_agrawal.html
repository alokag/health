These are evidence-based suggestions — please discuss with your doctor before starting any supplement or making major dietary changes.

🥗 Diet — Reduce Bad Cholesterol (LDL)

  • Avoid: Coconut oil, palm oil, ghee in excess, full-fat dairy, fried foods, bakery items (trans fats)
  • Use: Olive oil, mustard oil, cold-pressed groundnut oil (in moderation)
  • Eat more: Oats (beta-glucan lowers LDL), barley, psyllium husk (isabgol)
  • Add daily: 1 tsp flaxseeds + 5–6 walnuts (plant-based omega-3 lowers LDL)
  • Include: Rajma, chole, moong — soluble fibre binds cholesterol
  • Reduce: White rice, maida-based foods — replace with millets (jowar, bajra, ragi)
  • Eat more: Amla, berries, tomatoes — antioxidants protect arteries

💚 Diet — Raise Good Cholesterol (HDL)

  • Add: 1 tablespoon of extra-virgin olive oil daily
  • Include: Avocado (if available), nuts (almonds, walnuts) — raise HDL naturally
  • Drink: Green tea (2 cups/day) — shown to improve HDL levels
  • Avoid: Refined sugar, sweetened drinks — these lower HDL
  • Add: Fenugreek seeds (methi) soaked overnight — known to improve lipid profile
  • Spices: Turmeric + black pepper daily — anti-inflammatory, helps cholesterol

🏃 Exercise Plan

  • Aerobic (5 days/week): Brisk walking 45 min OR cycling 30 min — single most effective way to raise HDL
  • Strength training (2 days/week): Body weight exercises — improves insulin sensitivity
  • Yoga (daily, 20 min): Especially pranayama — reduces cortisol, improves lipid metabolism
  • Target: Minimum 150 min moderate exercise per week
  • Avoid: Prolonged sitting — take a 5-min walk every 90 minutes at your desk
  • Note: Regular aerobic exercise can raise HDL by 5–10% within 2–3 months

💊 Vitamin B12 (Vegetarian Priority)

  • Supplement: Methylcobalamin 1000 mcg/day (sublingual/oral) — discuss with doctor
  • Food sources: Fermented foods (idli, dosa, dhokla), fortified milk/cereal

  • Spirulina: Add 1 tsp spirulina powder to smoothie/juice — plant B12 source
  • Recheck B12 and MCV after 3 months of supplementation
  • Also check: Serum Folate — macrocytosis can be folate-related too
